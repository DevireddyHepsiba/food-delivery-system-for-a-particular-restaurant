Allergy Alert Information

Your safety is our top priority. Learn about common food allergies, symptoms to watch for, and how we handle allergy requests in our kitchen.

Important: Always inform your server about any food allergies before ordering. While we take precautions, we cannot guarantee a completely allergen-free environment.

Peanuts

One of the most common and potentially severe food allergies. Peanuts can cause life-threatening reactions in sensitive individuals.

Symptoms

  • Hives
  • Swelling
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Anaphylaxis

Common Items Containing Peanuts

  • Peanut butter
  • Satay sauce
  • Some baked goods
  • Asian dishes
  • Some sauces and dressings

Eggs

Egg allergies are common in children but may be outgrown. Both the yolk and white can cause reactions.

Symptoms

  • Skin reactions
  • Digestive problems
  • Nasal congestion
  • Respiratory issues

Common Items Containing Eggs

  • Mayonnaise
  • Baked goods
  • Pasta
  • Some sauces
  • Breaded foods

Fish & Shellfish

Fish and shellfish allergies are often lifelong and can develop in adulthood. Reactions can be severe.

Symptoms

  • Hives or rash
  • Swelling of face/lips
  • Dizziness
  • Anaphylaxis

Common Items Containing Fish/Shellfish

  • Seafood dishes
  • Some Asian sauces
  • Surimi (imitation crab)
  • Some salad dressings
  • Fish sauce

Gluten

Gluten intolerance includes celiac disease and non-celiac gluten sensitivity, requiring strict avoidance.

Symptoms

  • Digestive issues
  • Headaches
  • Fatigue
  • Joint pain

Common Items Containing Gluten

  • Bread and pasta
  • Beer
  • Soy sauce
  • Many processed foods
  • Some soups and sauces

Dairy

Dairy allergies involve the immune system reacting to milk proteins, different from lactose intolerance.

Symptoms

  • Hives
  • Wheezing
  • Vomiting
  • Digestive distress

Common Items Containing Dairy

  • Milk, cheese, butter
  • Cream-based soups
  • Many baked goods
  • Some processed meats
  • Chocolate

Tree Nuts

Includes allergies to almonds, walnuts, cashews, and other tree nuts. Often lifelong and potentially severe.

Symptoms

  • Abdominal pain
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Itchy mouth/throat
  • Anaphylaxis

Common Items Containing Tree Nuts

  • Nut butters
  • Pesto
  • Some desserts
  • Some Asian dishes
  • Some cereals

Our Allergy Prevention Measures

Strict Handwashing

All staff follow rigorous handwashing protocols before handling any food items.

Dedicated Utensils

Separate cooking and serving utensils for allergy-sensitive orders.

Detailed Menus

Comprehensive ingredient lists available for all menu items upon request.

Trained Staff

All employees receive regular allergy awareness training.

Emergency Information

1

Recognize Symptoms

Be aware of signs like difficulty breathing, swelling, hives, dizziness, or vomiting after eating.

2

Use Epinephrine

If prescribed, use epinephrine auto-injector immediately for severe reactions.

3

Call Emergency Services

Even if symptoms improve after epinephrine, emergency care is still needed.

4

Inform Restaurant Staff

Let us know immediately so we can assist and prevent further exposure.
